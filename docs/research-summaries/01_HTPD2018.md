# Strait, HTPD 2018 — Measuring Non-axisymmetric Fields from Internal and External Sources in the DIII-D Tokamak

## Document overview

- **Title:** "Measuring Non-axisymmetric Fields from Internal and External Sources in the DIII-D Tokamak"
- **Authors:** E.J. Strait (1) and R.M. Sweeney (2). Affiliations given as (1) General Atomics, (2) ITER Organization.
- **Venue/date:** Presented at the 22nd Topical Conference on High Temperature Plasma Diagnostics (HTPD), San Diego, CA, April 16–19, 2018. PDF creation date 2018-04-14, created in PowerPoint.
- **Length:** 38 pages (slide deck), including section-divider slides and an "EXTRAS" backup section (slides 35–38).
- **Purpose:** Presents a diagnostic/analysis *method* that uses magnetic field sensors located between the plasma and the wall to distinguish non-axisymmetric magnetic fields arising from *internal* sources (plasma modes) versus *external* sources (induced wall currents, non-axisymmetric coils). The core physics idea is that spatial phase shifts between the normal (B_r) and tangential (B_θ) field components identify which side of a source the measurement is on. The deck demonstrates the method on (a) a 2/1 tearing mode that grows and locks, and (b) an n=1 applied C-coil field with no plasma.
- **Structure (numbered sections):** (1) Introduction; (2) Magnetic Diagnostics; (3) Analysis Method; (4) Tearing Mode Locking (B_r & B_θ); (5) Tearing Mode Lock (B_wall & B_plasma); (6) n=1 Applied Field (no plasma); (7) Conclusions; then EXTRAS.

**Important caveat for the demo project:** This document does NOT describe the SLCONTOUR, MODESPEC, or OMFIT magnetics tools by name. It is a methods/diagnostics talk about decomposing magnetic signals into plasma vs. wall (internal vs. external) contributions. Its relevance is to the *underlying physics and plot styles* (B_r/B_θ spatial maps, time evolution of mode amplitude and phase, tearing-mode locking) that those tools also visualize.

## Analysis methods / math

(Section 3, slides 12–15.)

- **Scalar-potential / Laplace foundation (slides 5, 12):** The magnetic field between the plasma and the conductors is represented by a scalar potential obeying Laplace's equation, ∇²Φ = 0, with B = ∇Φ. Φ specified on a closed surface uniquely determines Φ and B inside (if no currents inside), and equivalently defines a virtual surface current that produces the same interior B. References cited: A. Boozer, Nuclear Fusion 55, 025001 (2015); J.D. Jackson, Classical Electrodynamics (1962).
- **Cylindrical-harmonic expansion ("straight tokamak" limit, slide 12):** Solutions for a cylindrical current distribution at r = a are expanded in cylindrical harmonics. The scalar potential is written (schematically) as
  Φ(r,θ,φ) = Σ_{m,n} ±A_{m,n} (r/a)^{±m} e^{imθ} e^{inφ},
  with upper/lower signs applying to measurements at r < a vs. r > a. The radial and poloidal fields follow from B = ∇Φ:
  - B_r(r,θ,φ) = Σ_{m,n} m A_{m,n} (r/a)^{±m−1} e^{imθ} e^{inφ}
  - B_θ(r,θ,φ) = Σ_{m,n} ± i m A_{m,n} (r/a)^{±m−1} e^{imθ} e^{inφ}
  Note the explicit caveat (slide 12): "The basis functions do NOT necessarily correspond to plasma modes."
- **Internal/external separation via the B_θ–B_r phase relation (slide 13):** Data are fitted to cylindrical harmonics, B_r^meas(θ,φ) = Σ_{m,n} b_r(m,n) e^{imθ} e^{inφ} (and similarly for B_θ). To satisfy Laplace's equation, b_θ(m,n) = ±i b_r(m,n), where "–" applies to sources at r < a and "+" applies to sources at r > a. Internal (r<a) and external (r>a) contributions for each (m,n) are recovered by:
  - b_r,int = (b_r + i b_θ)/2 ; b_r,ext = (b_r − i b_θ)/2
  - b_θ,int = −i b_r,int ; b_θ,ext = +i b_r,ext (with "flip the signs of i for m<0")
  - Total: b_r = b_r,int + b_r,ext ; b_θ = b_θ,int + b_θ,ext
  Then the (m,n) terms are summed to recover B_r(θ,φ) and B_θ(θ,φ).
- **Fitting via the "Normal Equations" method (slide 14):** A matrix A_{jk} of orthogonal basis functions evaluated at sensors j predicts the sensor measurement vector S_j = Σ_k A_jk B_k given basis coefficients B_k. The least-squares fit uses the pseudo-inverse: **B = (Aᵀ A)⁻¹ Aᵀ S**. The fitted function can be reconstructed at any spatial location from the coefficients B_k.
  - **Basis set:** cylindrical Fourier harmonics in φ and θ, δB(φ,θ) = Σ_{nm} b exp(inφ + imθ).
  - **Basis matrix for differential pairs** at [φ_{j,1},θ_j] and [φ_{j,2},θ_j]: A_jk = ⟨exp(inφ_{j,1} + imθ_j)⟩ − ⟨exp(inφ_{j,2} + imθ_j)⟩, where ⟨…⟩ indicates averaging over sensor areas and k indexes the (n,m) harmonic combinations.
  - **Figure of merit:** "SVD condition number of A is figure of merit for the validity of the decomposition" (slide 14).
- **Phase-shift physics (slide 4):** For a current sheet at x=0 periodic in z: B_x is continuous across x=0 but B_z reverses sign across x=0; B_z is phase-shifted in the z-direction by +90º at the right of the source and −90º at the left. Hence phase shifts distinguish which side of the source is being measured.
- **Special cases m=0 / n=0 (slide 15):**
  - m≠0, n=0 (shaped cross-section tokamak): internal vs. external distinguished by phase shifts between δB_θ and δB_r; possible application to equilibrium identification, VDEs.
  - m=0, n≠0 ("linked mirror"/"bumpy torus"): δB_θ is zero, but internal vs. external distinguished by phase shifts between δB_φ and δB_r; tokamak coils may generate non-resonant m=0 harmonics needing δB_φ data.
  - m=0, n=0 (straight circular tokamak): trivial — internal currents produce only δB_θ, external currents only δB_φ; no asymmetry, no phase shift to analyze.
- **Harmonics actually fitted in the example cases:**
  - Tearing-mode (plasma/wall) decomposition: "Harmonics fitted: n=1, m=[1, 3, 5]" (slide 23).
  - C-coil vacuum field: "Fits to the vacuum field shown here used m = [−2, 0, +2]" (slide 27).
- **Future improvement to the fitting (slides 33–34):** Currently B_r and B_tang are fitted separately then combined, which *assumes* the result satisfies Laplace's equation (OK for a plasma mode + its wall currents, but possibly not in general, especially with sparse diagnostics and differing B_r/B_tang structure). Proposed next step: fit B_r and B_tang *simultaneously* with separate basis functions for plasma and external fields, each indexed by m, n, and source (internal/external), guaranteeing Laplace consistency. Also: develop basis functions matched to observed/MHD-modeled mode structures; compare measured wall current during locking to models/MHD simulation; extend to non-axisymmetric coil + plasma-response separation.

## Visualizations to reproduce

All amplitude plots in the deck are in **Gauss (G)**. Two recurring plot types:

1. **2-D spatial maps (poloidal vs. toroidal contour maps).** Axes: horizontal φ (deg) from 0 to 360 (ticks 0, 90, 180, 270, 360); vertical θ (deg). For B_r/B_θ tearing-mode maps the θ axis runs roughly −40 to +40 deg (tick labels −40, −20, 0, 20, 40 appear). Color scale bars are labeled with field amplitude in Gauss; for the tearing-mode maps the color range is about −50 to +50 G (labels 50 / 0 / −50 visible). For the C-coil maps the color range is larger, about −60 to +60 G (labels 60, 40, 20, 0, −20, −40, −60). Distinct instances:
   - δB_r and δB_θ "Before Locking," t = 2720 ms (slide 19): B_r "shielded out of the wall... by mode rotation"; B_θ "strong helical structure... mode plus in-phase eddy currents."
   - δB_r and δB_θ "After Locking," t = 2760 ms (slide 20): B_r "of mode has penetrated the wall"; B_r and B_θ "have similar helical structure... with 90º phase shift."
   - δB_wall and δB_plasma "Before Locking," t = 2720 ms (slide 24): B_wall "equal and opposite to B_plasma"; image current shields the rotating mode.
   - δB_wall and δB_plasma "After Locking," t = 2760 ms (slide 25): B_wall "has vanished... after mode rotation stops"; B_plasma "locked at fixed phase... but amplitude has grown."
   - δB_r / δB_θ from C-coil (slide 28): B_r up/down symmetric ("horizontal field of coils at the midplane"); B_θ up/down anti-symmetric ("vertical spreading of horizontal field"). Color range ±60 G.
   - δB_coil / δB_plasma from C-coil (slide 29): B_coil up/down symmetric, similar to B_r; residual B_plasma "~5 G," i.e. "External field is canceled to within ~10%."
   - Backup "During Locking" maps for B_r/B_θ (slide 37) and B_wall/B_plasma (slide 38).

2. **Time-evolution panels: amplitude (G) and phase φ (deg) vs. time (ms).** Time axis 2700–2800 ms (ticks 2700, 2720, 2740, 2760, 2780, 2800). Amplitude axes about −40 to +40 G; phase axes 0–360 deg (ticks 0, 90, 180, 270, 360). Vertical reference lines at 2720 ms and 2760 ms recur. Instances:
