## Document overview
- Peer-reviewed conference paper (21st HTPD, Madison WI, June 2016). Authors: E. J. Strait (GA), J. D. King (GA), J. M. Hanson (Columbia), N. C. Logan (PPPL). 4 pages.
- Purpose: describe **hardware and software techniques used at DIII-D to condition individual magnetic signals and estimate the spatial structure of non-axisymmetric "3D" plasma features** from an ensemble of discrete measurements. Applications: detection of non-rotating MHD instabilities, plasma control, validation of 3D equilibrium models.
- This is the authoritative concise reference behind the OMFIT magnetics module's prep/fit method.

## Magnetic sensors / hardware
- A recent upgrade gives **2-axis measurements of the local magnetic field tangential (B_p) and normal (B_r) to the wall at ~64 locations** (Fig. 1).
- Sensors used in **toroidally separated differential pairs** measuring **B₁₂ = B(φ₁,θ) − B(φ₂,θ)** to minimize the axisymmetric contribution. This allows larger gain / better digitizer-resolution use (typically ×40 vs. channels measuring the total field) and reduces common-mode noise.
- Some sensors acquired individually as well as in pairs.
- Signals of individual sensors or pairs are **electronically integrated and digitized at a typical rate of 20 or 50 kHz**.
- Typical 3D amplitudes are **~10⁻³ to 10⁻⁵ of the total magnetic field** — small, slow time variation, challenging to measure.
- HFS sensors at 90° < θ < 270° are on a vertical wall (cylindrical harmonics in φ and z, a "flat" representation); spherical harmonics tested for LFS sensors.

## Analysis methods / math
- **Signal conditioning** (Sec. II): offset subtraction removes integrator drift and residual pickup of the axisymmetric field. For a transient event, a constant offset measured before the event is subtracted: ΔB₁₂(t) = B₁₂(t) − B₁₂(t₀). When only the AC part is needed, a **high-pass filter or synchronous detection** eliminates offset; signal also low-pass filtered (simple averaging or a time-domain digital filter) to reduce noise.
- **Synchronous detection**: for a periodic signal generated by an applied field rotating toroidally (or alternating polarity) at fixed frequency, a **running Fourier analysis at that frequency** yields an even cleaner signal than high-pass filtering. Needs no a-priori frequency if a reference (e.g., coil current) is available.
- Real-time/feedback control requires *causal* algorithms for offset subtraction, filtering, Fourier analysis.
- **Direct coupling correction**: must remove direct coupling from active coils to isolate plasma contribution. Low-frequency coupling measured via DC coil pulses; for AC, use frequency-domain transfer function (coil currents at several frequencies) or time-domain response function (step-function coil currents).
- **Spatial analysis ("normal equations" method, Sec. III)**: fit data to a functional form via a **design/basis matrix A** of orthogonal basis functions evaluated at each sensor (possibly differential pairs), so **A·B = S** (S = sensor measurements vector, B = basis-function coefficients). Least-squares fit: **B = (AᵀA)⁻¹AᵀS**. Detection matrix **D = (AᵀA)⁻¹Aᵀ** computed once per sensor/basis set.
- **Cylindrical Fourier harmonics**: δB(φ,θ) = Σ bₙₘ exp(i n φ − i m θ), bₙₘ complex amplitudes, m a geometrical poloidal angle. A plasma mode need not correspond to a single basis function. For a **toroidal array only m=0** is considered; for sensor pairs n=0 is automatically eliminated; fits to arrays of individual sensors must include n=0.
- **SVD conditioning**: A = U W Vᵀ; **condition number κ(A) = max(wᵢ)/min(wᵢ)**; large κ ⇒ basis functions poorly detected / under-constrained. Remedy: choose a smaller basis (omit high-order harmonics) — **upper limits n ≤ 2 and m ≤ 5 in Fig. 1 were set by requiring κ(A) < 10.** Alternative: zero the smallest singular values and rebuild a reduced-rank detection matrix D.
- **Space/time SVD (biorthogonal decomposition)**: decomposing the full sensor-data-vs-time matrix via SVD yields left-singular vectors = normalized spatial structures and right-singular vectors = temporal evolution. Largest singular values capture dominant behavior compactly; zeroing small ones filters noise. Replacing time with another independent variable separates plasma modes by their dependence on that variable.

## Digest
This is the authoritative method paper: DIII-D has ~64 two-axis (B_p, B_r) sensors used as toroidally-separated differential pairs to cancel the axisymmetric field, integrated and digitized at 20/50 kHz. Spatial structure is recovered by least-squares "normal equations" fitting to a cylindrical-Fourier basis δB(φ,θ)=Σbₙₘexp(inφ−imθ), with SVD used both to condition the design matrix (κ(A)<10 forces n≤2, m≤5) and to do biorthogonal space/time decomposition (first 2 singular values ≈98% energy for an n=1 mode). Signal conditioning progresses raw→offset-subtract→smooth+high-pass→synchronous Fourier filter (illustrated for a 20 Hz applied n=1 field). The four figures define the canonical visualizations to reproduce: a φ–θ δB_p contour with sensor overlay (locked m/n=2/1), a 5-stage signal-conditioning stack, a toroidal-mode amplitude/phase/contour panel, and an SVD energy + time-vector pair.
